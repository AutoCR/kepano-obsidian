One-line takeaway: **HDP argues diffusion planning becomes truly strong for E2E AD only when trajectory representation, loss design, data scale, and RL post-training are all tuned for real-world deployment.**

# Core takeaways
- Argues diffusion planners were underperforming mainly because of poor design choices rather than a bad paradigm.
- Studies trajectory representation, loss design, and data scaling together.
- Validates the final system with real-vehicle deployment rather than only public benchmarks.

# Method summary
HDP uses an industrial perception backbone with a diffusion planning head and carefully studies how diffusion loss geometry, velocity-vs-waypoint representation, and data scale affect performance. A hybrid loss and safety-aware RL post-training produce the final Hyper Diffusion Planner.

# Benchmarks / results
The paper emphasizes real-vehicle testing across 200 km and multiple urban scenarios, claiming roughly a 10× improvement over the base model after the full HDP stack and RL post-training are applied.

# Relation to the score-based E2E AD lineage
HDP is an important large-scale diffusion paper in this area. Compared with Hydra-MDP or SparseDriveV2, it argues that a relatively clean diffusion planner can become competitive when scaled and tuned properly.

# Caveats
- Relies heavily on proprietary data and deployment infrastructure.
- Direct apples-to-apples comparison to public benchmark methods is harder.
