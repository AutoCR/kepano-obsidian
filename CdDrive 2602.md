One-line takeaway: **CdDrive tries to get the best of both worlds: keep strong static anchors for easy scenes, and add diffusion-refined candidates only where scene interaction demands it.**

# Core takeaways
- Keeps original vocabulary candidates instead of always overwriting them with refined ones.
- Adds diffusion-refined candidates only when scene interaction requires adaptation.
- Uses a shared scorer to choose among static and refined candidates jointly.

# Method summary
CdDrive starts from a predefined trajectory vocabulary, preserves those original anchors, and augments them with scene-adaptive candidates generated by vocabulary-conditioned diffusion denoising. HATNA smooths the injected noise over time and horizon, and a shared scoring module ranks the combined candidate set.

# Benchmarks / results
On NAVSIM v1, CdDrive reports about 89.2 PDMS; on NAVSIM v2, it reports about 86.4 EPDMS, outperforming several pure vocabulary and diffusion baselines in the paper’s comparisons.

# Relation to the score-based E2E AD lineage
CdDrive sits between pure Hydra/SparseDrive-style scoring and fully generative refinement methods, making it a representative hybrid candidate-set paper.

# Caveats
- More system complexity than pure scoring-only methods.
- Benefits depend on balancing preserved anchors and refined candidates well.
