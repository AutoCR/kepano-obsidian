One-line takeaway: **HAD makes diffusion planning easier to optimize by splitting planning into coarse and fine stages, then trains it with decoupled RL rewards instead of one monolithic score.**

# Core takeaways
- Splits diffusion planning into coarse intention establishment and fine local refinement.
- Uses decoupled RL rewards instead of one monolithic scalar objective.
- Shows strong gains on both NAVSIM and HUGSIM.

# Method summary
HAD decomposes planning into a hierarchical diffusion pipeline: a coarse stage proposes intention-level trajectories and a fine stage refines them locally. A structure-preserved expansion step preserves kinematic plausibility, while metric-decoupled policy optimization provides clearer RL signals for safety, progress, and related objectives.

# Benchmarks / results
On NAVSIM, HAD reports 90.2 PDMS on v1 and 88.6 EPDMS on v2; on HUGSIM, it reports 47.5 route completion and 30.8 HD-Score for the larger model.

# Relation to the score-based E2E AD lineage
HAD is a direct continuation of the diffusion-based score-planning line after DiffusionDriveV2, but introduces hierarchy to manage the large action space more effectively.

# Caveats
- Engineering complexity is high.
- Hierarchy and RL contributions are somewhat entangled in the final gains.
