One-line takeaway: **HMAD combines DiffusionDrive-style anchored proposal generation with Hydra-style simulation-supervised multi-target scoring, showing the continued strength of decoupled proposal-and-score planning.**

# Core takeaways
- Decouples planning into proposal generation plus multi-criteria learned scoring.
- Uses anchored queries and iterative offsets to produce many candidates.
- Learns the scorer directly from simulator-derived metrics including collision, drivable area, and comfort.

# Method summary
HMAD uses a BEVFormer backbone to generate scene features, then decodes candidate trajectories from learnable anchors refined through offset prediction. A separate scorer predicts multiple simulator-style metrics for each candidate so the final trajectory is selected through learned scoring rather than simple heuristics.

# Benchmarks / results
The paper reports 44.5% driving score on the CVPR 2025 private challenge test set and about 65.94 EPDMS on a NAVSIM-v2-style warmup benchmark.

# Relation to the score-based E2E AD lineage
HMAD is a fusion of the two main branches: Hydra-style simulator-supervised scoring plus DiffusionDrive-style anchored proposal generation.

# Caveats
- Heavier and more challenge-engineered than simpler score-based methods.
- Final quality still depends on proposal coverage.
